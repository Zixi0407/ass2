THE ECONOMIC FREEDOM INDEX

What are economic freedom and the economic freedom index?

Economic freedom is the basic right of people to control their own labour and property. In an economically free society, people there are free, and government allow capital and goods to move freely. The economic freedom index is a measurement of economic freedom level. The higher index, the more economic freedom that place is.

Top 3 economic entities in the economic freedom index with GDP and Population

Relationship with GDP & population

Economic freedom brings greater prosperity. In economic GDP is an important measurement to measure prosperity and the population is also important for the labour force. However, from the isotype chart, we can see that there is no significant relationship between population or GDP with the economic freedom index.

The reason why is that GDP is not the only measurement, instead, if we add population in the calculation. There is another measurement GDP per capita. It is a better measurement to measure the people's living standard and prosperity.

The stacked bar chart

The 12 factors are mentioned in the last graph. Among these 12 factors, it can be divided into 4 pillars: Rule of Law (property right, government integrity, judicial effectiveness), Government Size (government spending, tax burden, fiscal health), Regulatory Efficiency (business freedom, labour freedom, monetary freedom), Open Markets (trade freedom, investment freedom, financial freedom).

The six countries have the relatively same distribution of the 4 pillars. It is understandable that being in the top 6 means have an economic freedom index larger than 80/100, which is a high score. Therefore, if any part of these 6 entities has a low score, it is impossible to get into this position.
